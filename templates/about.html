A writing instructor introduced me to the idea of weasel words. These are words that often enable, accompany or promote bad writing. They add little to your prose and suck vitality away. In general, I try to avoid using them when I can.

This website houses a simple tool to help writers find weasel words in their prose. On the home page of this site is a web form. If you paste text into that form and press submit, the site will create a report on how often you used each of the weasel words (and where in your text they appeared).

A few notes are important before you use this site. First, weasel words aren't always bad to use. If you think a weasel word is helping you make your case, go with God and keep in place. How you use the weasel word list is up to you.

Second, there are a few technical details people should know. First, since this is a hobby site, its underlying technology is modest. If you find that the site is unavailable, try again later. Also, to make the software faster, we made a few simplifications in how we count weasel words. In the majority of cases, your counts will be exactly correct. But there are some edge cases where a count might be one or two off. The longer your input text, the more likely your counts might be off by one.
